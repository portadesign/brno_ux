Homepage › Work › Right to work in Czechia

Detail tématu (L3)

EU and Schengen citizens can work in Czechia freely. Non-EU citizens need a residence permit plus a work permit — or one of the combined cards (Employee Card, EU Blue Card, ICT Card). Recognition of foreign professional qualifications is a separate process — start early.

About working as a foreigner

Your legal route depends on citizenship and intended duration. Most expats end up holding either an Employee Card (long-term work residence) or, if highly qualified, an EU Blue Card.

  • EU/EEA & Swiss citizens — no work permit needed, free movement applies.
  • Long-term or permanent residents — already authorised to work; no additional permit.
  • Employee Card — the standard combined work + residence permit for non-EU specialists.
  • EU Blue Card — for highly qualified (university degree + salary ≥1.5× national average).
  • ICT Card — intra-company transfers from non-EU branches.
