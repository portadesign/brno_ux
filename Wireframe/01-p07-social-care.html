Detail tématu (L3)

Czech social system supports families, seniors and people in material need — through state benefits, allowances and assistance programmes. Most apply through Úřad práce; the city's Social Services Department (OSP) handles social work and city-level support.

About social care

The state-run social system has three pillars: state social support (family allowances), material need support (low-income help) and disability/long-term care. Brno's OSP coordinates city-level social work and outreach for foreigners.

  • Child allowance (přídavek na dítě) up to 2,100 CZK/month per child for low-income families.
  • Housing allowance (příspěvek na bydlení) — partial rent coverage for low-income tenants.
  • Parental allowance (rodičovský příspěvek) — 350,000 CZK total, drawable until child is 4.
  • Maternity benefit (PPM) — 28 weeks paid leave from social insurance.
  • Material need benefits — last-resort safety net (housing supplement, immediate assistance).
