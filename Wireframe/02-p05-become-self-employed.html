Detail tématu (L3)

Self-employment in Czechia (OSVČ — Osoba Samostatně Výdělečně Činná) is straightforward and tax-favourable. The Trade Licensing Office (Živnostenský úřad) issues your trade licence in days — and from there you manage taxes, social and health insurance contributions directly.

About self-employment (OSVČ)

Czechia treats freelancers as OSVČ — a flexible legal status with low setup cost. Foreigners need a valid residence permit (long-term or permanent) before applying. Trade types determine which qualifications are required.

  • Four trade types: volné (free), vázané (bound), řemeslné (regulated craft), koncesované (concession).
  • Trade licence fee: 1,000 CZK one-time. Apply at the Trade Office with residence permit.
  • Monthly minimums (2026): ~3,500 CZK social + health insurance combined.
  • 60/40 expenses scheme: deduct 60% of revenue as expenses without proof — popular shortcut.
  • Flat-tax (paušální daň): single monthly payment covering all obligations; eligible under 2M CZK revenue.

First year OSVČ?

Lower minimum insurance applies in your first year as OSVČ. Check thresholds and act before the first payment deadline (8th of each month).
