Brno's UNESCO heritage, functionalist masterpieces and underground gems — all curated on the city's official tourism portal. From Villa Tugendhat to the secret tunnels beneath the Old Town, the city rewards both casual walkers and architecture lovers.

About Brno's architecture

Brno is a UNESCO city of design and one of Europe's strongest functionalist heritage capitals. Add medieval Špilberk Castle, baroque churches, and a network of underground tunnels — and you have layers of history visible on a single afternoon walk.

  • UNESCO: Villa Tugendhat (1930, Mies van der Rohe) — UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Functionalism: ~400 functionalist buildings — Hotel Avion, Zeman Café, Era Café.
  • Medieval: Špilberk Castle (13th c.), St Peter & Paul Cathedral, Old Town Hall.
  • Underground: Brno Labyrinth (10 km of tunnels), 10-Z nuclear bunker, Capuchin Crypt.
  • Walking tours: Daily English-language guided tours via TIC BRNO and private guides.
