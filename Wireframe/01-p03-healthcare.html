In case of emergency

Know what to do, who to call, and where to go. Czech emergency care is provided to anyone in a life-threatening situation, regardless of insurance.

When to call an ambulance

Life-threatening situations: chest pain, severe injuries, unconsciousness, breathing difficulties, severe bleeding.

Emergency phone numbers

  • 112 — Universal emergency (EN-friendly)
  • 155 — Ambulance
  • 150 — Fire

Health insurance

The Czech system has two tracks: public (state-run, for residents with employment or family ties) and commercial (for long-term visa holders, dependents, etc.). Your residence permit determines which one applies.

  • Public — Employed, self-employed, EU family members, students with S1/E106, refugees with temporary protection
  • Commercial — Long-term visa without employment, non-EU dependents, short-stay visitors
