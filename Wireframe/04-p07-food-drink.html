About food in Brno

Brno sits at the gateway to Moravian wine country and has one of the densest microbrewery counts in CZ. Czech home-cooking lunches (denní menu) stay around 150-180 CZK; upscale dining 400-700 CZK per main course.

  • Czech cuisine: Pork-knuckle, dumplings, svíčková — classic spots: Pegas, Lokál, Stopkova plzeňská pivnice.
  • Beer scene: Pegas, Starobrno, Černá Hora plus 20+ microbreweries.
  • Wine: Moravian wine region just south — Pálava, Mikulov, Valtice; wine bars in city centre.
  • International: Asian (Vietnamese pho everywhere), Italian, Lebanese, Indian, Mexican, Georgian.
  • Vegan/veggie: Forest, Plevel, Zelený Pták — vibrant plant-based scene.
