Detail tématu (L3)

Once you sign an employment contract in Czechia, you're subject to Czech labour law — favourable to employees but with specific quirks around contract types, gross-net calculations and termination notice.

About Czech employment law

Czech labour law (Zákoník práce) heavily favours employees. Three contract types cover different work intensities, and the tax/insurance system runs through automatic deductions by the employer.

  • HPP (hlavní pracovní poměr) — standard open-ended employment with full benefits.
  • DPP (dohoda o provedení práce) — work agreement, max 300 hours/year per employer.
  • DPČ (dohoda o pracovní činnosti) — work activity agreement, max 20 hours/week average.
  • Net pay is about 73–77% of gross for typical incomes after tax, social and health insurance.
  • Notice period: 2 months on HPP; severance 1–3 months if employer terminates without cause.
